Brazil

Overall Income Inequality

Gini coefficient for household equivalised income from SEDLAC. The data are derived from the PNAD household survey (replaced from 2016 with the 'Continuous PNAD' or PNADC). Over the period covered, the survey underwent several methodological changes; these are indicated within SEDLAC as breaks in the series, but with only very limited descriptions: the latest data, from 2016 onwards, is specified as including implicit rent, suggesting that this was not included in earlier data, or not included consistently. The data from 2004 is specified as including the 'Rural North', again suggesting that earlier figures exclude this region. We construct the final series by linking the subseries across these breaks. No figures are available between 1990 and 1993 in the gap between the first subseries ending and the second starting; here we link the series by assuming there was no intervening change in inequality between 1990 and 1993. For a discussion of the data sources on which SEDLAC is based and their limitations, see Gasparini, Cruces & Tornarolli (2011).

Top Income Shares

Pre-tax national income share held by top percentile from WID.world. Data relates to individuals over age 20, assuming resources are split equally within couples.

Poverty

Gini coefficient for household equivalised income from SEDLAC. The data are derived from the PNAD household survey (replaced from 2016 with the 'Continuous PNAD' or PNADC). The data from 2004 is specified as including the 'Rural North', suggesting that earlier figures exclude this region. Percentage of individuals below 50 per cent of median household per capita income from SEDLAC, linked at 2004 to the “New PNAD” data series, linked at 1992 to the earlier “PNAD” series (on the assumption of no change between 1990 and 1992). No figures are available between 1990 and 1992 in the gap between the first subseries ending and the second starting; here we link the series by assuming there was no intervening change in inequality between 1990 and 1992. Earlier figures from before 1990, available in previous vintages of the dataset, were not included in the 2020 release; we extend the series back to 1981 by linking to data taken from the 2018 release for this period.

Earnings Dispersion

Gini coefficient for labour earnings in six main metropolitan regions, persons aged 15-60, from Neri (2010).
